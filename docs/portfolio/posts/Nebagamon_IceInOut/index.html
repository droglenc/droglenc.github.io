Summary
  • Lake Nebagamon, in Douglas County, is 986 acres with a maximum depth of 56 feet.
  • Ice-in and out dates recorded from 1943 to 2023.
  • The average ice-in date is Nov 29 and the average ice-out date is Apr 19 (see Figure 1).
  • The range for both ice-in (Nov 7 to Dec 26) and ice-out (Mar 13 to May 14) dates is considerable, at approximately six to eight weeks (see Figure 1).
  • There are weak indications for a slightly later ice-in date and, possibly, a slightly earlier ice-out date in the last two decades (see Figure 2).
  • Since 1943 the average days of ice coverage was 141 days, though the range was considerable from 105 to 174 days (see Figure 3).
  • There is an indication that the length of ice duration has declined, on average, by a little over a week in the last two decades (see Figure 4).
  • Eight of the ten shortest ice duration seasons have occurred since 2000 (see Table 1), whereas only one of the ten longest ice duration seasons have occurred in that same time period (see Table 2).
